How to Fill Out the Risk Register

Risk ID
Format: [Letter].[R#] — e.g. A.R1, A.R2. The letter matches your audit designation. Each distinct risk gets its own number. Controls under the same risk share the risk number: A.R1.C1, A.R1.C2…
Risk Description
Line 1: [Risk ID] - [Short Name]
Line 2 — 3-part VIA format:
(DESCRIPTION) [what is failing or not occurring],
(ROOT CAUSE) due to [specific control gap or process failure — not a symptom],
(IMPACT) resulting in [concrete business consequence — financial, regulatory, operational, or reputational].

Example: "A.R1 - Alarm Monitoring
(DESCRIPTION) Alarms not generating to signal stop of network traffic,
(ROOT CAUSE) due to network equipment and circuits being inappropriately configured,
(IMPACT) resulting in network interruption and poor customer experience."
FROST Sub-Category
F = Financial (billing, fraud, reporting, cash)
R = Regulatory & Legal (compliance, privacy, licenses)
O = Operational (process, people, continuity, sales)
S = Strategic & Reputational (customer exp, governance)
T = Technology & InfoSec (access, data, cyber, change mgmt)
Pick the sub-category that best describes what could go wrong.
Likelihood × Impact → Risk Level
Likelihood: Likely (will probably occur) · Possible (may occur) · Unlikely (not expected)
Impact: High (major damage/regulatory) · Medium (moderate disruption) · Low (minor)
Risk Level is auto-calculated. Likely+High=High · Possible+High=High · Possible+Low=Low
Controls Tested
Format: [Risk ID].C[#] — WHO/WHAT/WHEN/WHERE/WHY
(WHO) [who performs the control]
(WHAT) [specific action taken]
(WHEN) [frequency — must match Control Frequency field]
(WHERE) [system or location where performed]
(WHY) to ensure [control objective / what it prevents]

Example: "A.R1.C1 — (WHO) Management and vendors (WHAT) test and approve equipment and circuits (WHEN) quarterly (WHERE) in the Central Office (WHY) to ensure alarms are generated at the NMCs."

If design gap found during walkthrough: enter exact phrase Missing Control - Control Gap and set Control Type accordingly. Stop testing.
Control Type
Preventative = stops the risk from occurring
Detective = identifies it after it occurs
Corrective = fixes/remediates it after detection
Paired with: Manual (human-executed) · Automated (system) · Hybrid
Use Missing Control - Control Gap when no adequate control exists. STOP testing that risk once this is identified.
Control Frequency
How often the control operates. Drives sample size requirements — Daily/continuous controls require larger samples (25 standard, 10 lower risk) than Annual (1 sample).
IIA Topical Requirements
Applies to Cybersecurity (Cyber.Gov/Risk/Ctrl.X) and Third-Party (TP.Gov/Risk/Ctrl.X) domain audits only. Use Not Applicable for most operational audits.

Data Sampling Plan
  • Define sampling approach for each in-scope control before fieldwork begins
  • VIA sampling table: 1–249 population → 25 samples; 250–999 → 40; 1,000+ → 60 (attribute sampling)
  • Use Haphazard for small populations or judgmental selections; document rationale
  • For monetary unit or statistical sampling, document the random seed, interval, and selection methodology
